WHAT TO DO
Bored and have nothing to do on your trip? Don’t worry, we’ll tell you the best things to do while your in Italy from exploring ancient history to enjoying modern culture and amazing food. Walk through the Colosseum and Roman Forum in Rome, where history comes to life, then toss a coin into the Trevi Fountain for good luck. Head to Florence to see world-famous art like Michelangelo’s David and stroll across the Ponte Vecchio. In Venice, ride a vaporetto along the Grand Canal and get lost in the city’s winding streets. Don’t forget to try authentic pizza in Naples, gelato anywhere you go, and take a day trip to the colorful villages of Cinque Terre. No matter your interests, Italy has something unforgettable waiting for you.
Along the Italian coast, the land loosens its grip and lets the sea take over. Beaches curve gently beneath pastel towns, where shuttered windows glow in the afternoon sun and the air smells faintly of salt and citrus. The water shifts through impossible shades—clear jade near the shore, deep sapphire farther out—lapping against smooth pebbles and pale sand. Wooden fishing boats rest like sunbathers pulled onto the beach, while laughter and conversation drift from umbrellas striped in blue and white.
Italy’s food is like tasting the soul of the country—rich, regional, and crafted with a deep respect for tradition. Each meal feels like a small celebration. In the north, creamy risottos and fresh pasta dishes like tagliatelle al ragù warm the table, while in the south, bright flavors of tomatoes, basil, and olive oil come alive in every bite.
